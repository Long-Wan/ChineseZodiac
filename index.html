Introduction

The Chinese zodiac is the classification scheme that assigns an animal to each year in a repeating twelve-year cycle; popular in many East Asian countries such as China, Vietnam, Korea, Japan, Philippines, Thailand and Taiwan.

A person's Zodiac sign is determined by the year he/she was born in. Each animal of the Zodiac is associated with several character traits and compatibility with other animals of the Zodiac.

The Great Race is the most popular origin story that explains the order of the Chinese Zodiac signs. The story tells of a race between the 12 animals, and the order that they finished the race determined their order on the Chinese Zodiac. The order is as follows:

  1. Rat
  2. Ox
  3. Tiger
  4. Rabbit
  5. Dragon
  6. Snake
  7. Horse
  8. Goat
  9. Monkey
  10. Rooster
  11. Dog
  12. Pig
